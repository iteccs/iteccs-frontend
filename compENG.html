Electrical & Computer Engineering

Intelligent systems that sense, compute, communicate, and act

Electrical & Computer Engineering connects advanced electronics, embedded computing, wireless communication, robotics, signal intelligence, photonics, power systems, and AI. Students learn how modern systems actually work — from custom chips and FPGA logic to 5G networks, radar, computer vision, autonomous drones, medical imaging, and intelligent power grids.

Autonomy & Robotics 5G & Secure Wireless Photonics & Sensing AI & Perception Power & Electrification
You will be able to:
• Design control systems for autonomous robots and aircraft
• Build embedded and FPGA-based computing platforms
• Engineer wireless, radar, optical, and satellite communication links
• Develop AI-driven sensing, perception, and decision systems
• Design power, energy, and electrification systems for the real world

Signals, Systems & Intelligent Foundations

The analytical core of modern ECE: modeling, stability, estimation, optimization.

  • Circuits, Devices and Fields
    Circuit theory, semiconductor devices, electromagnetics, and digital logic — the base layer of all electronic systems.
  • Signals and Systems
    Linear time-invariant systems, Fourier/Laplace/z-transforms, sampling, and system response in time and frequency.
  • Probability and Stochastic Processes for Engineers
    Random variables, stochastic processes, and noise modeling for sensing, communication, and estimation.
  • Linear System Theory
    State-space modeling, controllability, observability, stability, and feedback/observer design.
  • Estimation Theory and Applications
    Detection, parameter estimation, multivariate statistics, and inference in noisy environments for radar and communications.
  • Modern Convex Optimization
    Optimization algorithms (LP, QP, SOCP, SDP, ADMM) for control, signal processing, medical imaging, and machine learning.

Control, Autonomy & Robotics

Making machines stable, aware, adaptive, and self-directed.

  • Continuous Control Systems
    Classical and state-space control, stability, controllability, observability, and controller design.
  • Discrete-Time Control Systems
    Digital control modeling, sampling effects, z-transform methods, and implementation on embedded platforms.
  • Optimal Control and Estimation
    Linear quadratic control, Kalman filters, stochastic estimation, and performance-driven decision strategies.
  • Decentralized Control and Consensus in Multi-Agent Systems
    Distributed cooperation, consensus algorithms, scalability, and robustness across teams of agents.
  • Uncrewed Aerial Vehicle Systems and Control
    Guidance, navigation, stabilization, and autonomous flight control using onboard sensing and real-time estimation.
  • Foundations of Reinforcement Learning
    Dynamic programming, optimal control, deep RL, and policy optimization for autonomous decision-making.
  • Feedback Control in Biological Signaling Pathways
    How cells regulate themselves — biological signaling and adaptation seen through control theory.
  • Introduction to AI in Robotics & Intelligent Systems
    Robot kinematics, perception, learning-based control, human–robot teaming, and autonomy.
  • Modern Navigation Systems
    GPS, inertial navigation, terrain-aided navigation, sensor fusion, and multi-sensor state estimation.

Communication, Wireless, Networks & Radar

Moving information across air, fiber, orbit, and contested spectrum.

  • Communications Systems Engineering
    Analog and digital modulation/demodulation (AM, FM, PM, PSK, QAM), channel noise, and link performance.
  • Next Generation Telecommunications
    Broadband, fiber, wireless backbones, voice/data/video transport, privacy, encryption, and future architectures.
  • Computer and Data Communication Networks
    Network architecture, routing, transport protocols, wireless evolution, and secure end-to-end communication.
  • Next Generation Mobile Networks and Security
    4G/5G architectures, mmWave, edge compute, handover, network slicing, and wireless security strategies.
  • Wireless and Mobile Cellular Communications
    Radio access networks, interference management, multiple-access schemes, link budgets, and capacity enhancement.
  • Satellite Communications Systems
    Satellite link design, antennas and payloads, high-throughput architectures, and deep-space communication limits.
  • Radar Systems
    Radar waveforms, ambiguity functions, range/Doppler processing, detection, and system-level performance.
  • Radar Signal Processing
    Doppler processing, adaptive filtering, moving target indication, and tracking from real radar data.
  • Synthetic Aperture Radar
    High-resolution imaging, motion compensation, interferometry, spotlight/stripmap modes, and moving target detection.
  • Passive Emitter Geo-Location
    Locating RF emitters using time-difference of arrival, direction finding, and statistical estimation.
  • Digital Receiver Synchronization Techniques
    Carrier and symbol recovery, timing synchronization, and robust demodulator alignment for digital receivers.
  • Multirate Signal Processing
    Decimation, interpolation, polyphase filter banks, and efficient multirate architectures.
  • Error Control Coding
    Block codes, convolutional codes, turbo-like iterative decoding, and forward error correction for reliability.
  • Iterative Methods in Communication Systems
    Belief propagation, factor graphs, and iterative decoding schemes that push performance toward channel capacity.
  • Software-Defined Radio for Wireless Communications
    End-to-end wireless physical layer design on programmable radio platforms (FPGA / SDR), from baseband to RF.
  • Image Compression, Packet Video, and Video Processing
    Compression standards, motion estimation, packetized video transport, quality, resilience, and streaming at scale.

Signal Processing, Perception & Intelligent Sensing

Teaching machines to see, hear, interpret, and understand the world.

  • Digital Signal Processing
    Sampling, DFT/FFT, filtering, spectral analysis, and discrete-time system design.
  • Digital Signal Processing Lab / Applications of DSP
    Hands-on DSP implementation: deconvolution, beamforming, channel equalization, spectral estimation, and real-time filtering.
  • Adaptive Signal Processing
    Adaptive filters, LMS-like algorithms, interference cancellation, and channel equalization under changing conditions.
  • Advanced Digital Signal Processing
    High-resolution spectral estimation, optimal filters, subspace and least-squares techniques, matrix methods.
  • Machine Learning for Signal Processing
    Learning-based classification, denoising, retrieval, and interpretation of audio, speech, image, and video signals.
  • Deep Learning and Generative Artificial Intelligence
    Deep neural architectures, transformers, diffusion models, generative vision/audio models, and real deployment.
  • Deep Learning for Computer Vision
    Object detection, localization, segmentation, scene understanding, multimodal fusion, and robustness evaluation.
  • Machine Perception
    Multi-sensor perception (vision, radar, lidar), object tracking, scene understanding, and situational awareness for autonomy.
  • Speech Processing / Audio Signal Processing
    Speech production and perception, speech enhancement, coding, synthesis, recognition, and speaker ID.
  • Introduction to Digital Image and Video Processing
    Image/video sampling, filtering, feature extraction, motion estimation, stereo and depth, and texture analysis.
  • Image Processing & Analysis / Image Engineering
    Imaging physics, sensing hardware, enhancement, segmentation, compression, biomedical and remote-sensing applications.

RF, Photonics & Remote Sensing Hardware

The physical layer: fields, light, antennas, receivers, and high-frequency electronics.

  • Intermediate Electromagnetics
    Maxwell’s equations, propagation, reflection/refraction, transmission lines, waveguides, and radiation.
  • Electromagnetic Transmission Systems
    High-frequency transmission, impedance matching, scattering parameters, Smith Chart methods, and millimeter-wave behavior.
  • Principles of RF and Microwave Circuits / RF and Microwave Circuits
    S-parameters, amplifiers, mixers, oscillators, and passive/active microwave network design.
  • Microwave Systems & Receiver Design
    RF/microwave front ends, noise figure, linearity, dynamic range, and architectures for radar and surveillance systems.
  • Antenna Systems
    Dipoles, loops, phased arrays, beam shaping, mutual coupling, and antenna measurement.
  • Advanced Antenna Systems
    Array synthesis, leaky-wave structures, mutual coupling analysis, and full-array performance optimization.
  • Antenna Design for Space Systems
    Spaceborne antennas for large and small spacecraft; link budget constraints, thermal and mechanical limits, verification workflows.
  • Laser Fundamentals
    Laser gain, pumping, resonators, Gaussian beams, and the physics of light–matter interaction.
  • Laser Systems and Applications
    Nonlinear optics, interferometry, holography, precision sensing, and photonic instrumentation.
  • Fundamentals of Photonics and Fiber Optic Systems
    Guided-wave optics, polarization, optoelectronic components, detectors, and high-speed optical communication links.
  • Fourier Techniques in Optics
    Diffraction, spatial frequency analysis, holography, optical information processing, and imaging as a Fourier system.
  • Introduction to Optical Instruments
    Design and performance of lenses, microscopes, telescopes, interferometers, spectrometers, and scientific imaging systems.
  • Advanced Topics in Optical Medical Imaging
    Optical sensing in biomedicine: fluorescence, Raman, scattering, endoscopy, molecular contrast, and neurophotonics.
  • Introduction to EO/IR Systems
    Electro-optical / infrared sensing systems, radiation physics, detectors, automatic target recognition, and remote sensing.
  • Propagation and Atmospheric Effects at RF / Microwave / Millimeter Wave
    How the environment (atmosphere, rain, ionosphere) shapes RF and microwave link performance in terrestrial, airborne, and space systems.

Embedded Systems, FPGA & Advanced Computing Hardware

Where intelligence meets silicon, firmware, and real-time constraints.

  • Microprocessors for Robotic Systems
    Embedded compute platforms for robotics: sensor interfaces, actuation, and real-time control.
  • Embedded Microprocessor Systems / Embedded Systems Development Lab
    Hardware/software co-design, peripherals, timing, reliability, board bring-up, and full embedded product integration.
  • Computer Architecture
    Instruction sets, pipelining, hazards, caching, memory hierarchy, I/O, interrupts, DMA, and microarchitectural tradeoffs.
  • FPGA Design Using VHDL
    RTL design, synthesis, timing closure, verification, and deploying custom digital logic on FPGA hardware.
  • System-on-a-Chip FPGA Design Laboratory
    Custom SoC design, hardware acceleration, embedded processing, DSP pipelines, and Linux-on-FPGA integration.
  • Hardware Architectures for DSP Algorithms
    Mapping signal-processing algorithms into efficient hardware: pipelining, parallelism, systolic arrays, multi-rate/multichannel structures.
  • Digital VLSI System Design
    CMOS logic from gates to subsystems: combinational/sequential logic, synthesis, placement/routing, timing, full digital IC implementation.
  • Mixed-Mode VLSI Circuit Design
    Precision analog + digital on one chip: data converters, amplifiers, bias networks, layout for noise, robustness, and power.
  • Analog Electronic Circuit Design / Advanced Analog Electronic Circuit Design
    Amplifiers, oscillators, regulators, data-converter interfaces, SPICE-level behavior, and evaluation on real hardware.
  • High Speed Digital Design
    Signal integrity at high data rates: transmission lines, crosstalk, differential signaling, timing closure, and compliance at board level.
  • Measurement and Instrumentation
    Building custom instruments: sensing, data acquisition, conversion and conditioning, calibration, and full performance validation.
  • Introduction to the Solid State / Electronic Materials
    Crystal structure, semiconductor physics, band structure, thermal/mechanical/optical properties of electronic materials and interfaces.
  • Introduction to Electronic Packaging
    Thermal, mechanical, electrical, and environmental design of high-speed/high-frequency electronic assemblies and enclosures.

Power, Energy Systems & Electrification

Powering vehicles, infrastructure, autonomy, and the grid.

  • Introduction to Electric Power Systems
    Generation, transmission, transformers, load flow, fault analysis, reliability, and renewable integration into large-scale grids.
  • Electric Machines and Control for Electrification
    Electric motors and drives, motor control, power electronics for propulsion and automation, and model-based control for electrified systems.
  • Power System Control, Optimization, and Grid Management
    Grid stability, voltage and frequency control, dispatch, unit commitment, and economics of operating modern power networks.
  • Power Electronics / Applications of Power Electronics Design / Advanced Power Electronics
    Switching converters, EMI control, control loops, magnetics, PCB layout, thermal design, drive electronics, and high-efficiency energy conversion.
  • Microwave Systems & Receiver Design
    High-frequency receiver chains, oscillators, mixers, noise figure, and radar/surveillance front ends — critical in aerospace, defense, and resilient infrastructure.
  • Propagation and Atmospheric Effects at RF / Microwave / Millimeter Wave
    How the atmosphere, terrain, and environment shape high-frequency communication, sensing, and power-relevant remote monitoring links.
Electrical & Computer Engineering prepares you to design the nervous system of modern technology: from embedded intelligence and autonomous flight control to 5G security, photonic sensing, medical imaging, satellite links, high-speed computing hardware, and intelligent power grids.
