Chemical & Biomolecular Engineering

Engineering matter, energy, and life

This area combines chemistry, biology, physics, computation, and systems design. Students learn how to model reactions, move mass and energy, design biomaterials, deliver drugs, build clean energy systems, and shape the next generation of health and sustainability technologies.

Biotech & Medicine Energy & Sustainability Computation & AI Advanced Materials
You will be able to:
• Design and optimize chemical and biological processes
• Model transport, reaction, and delivery inside real systems (devices, tissues, reactors)
• Build intelligent, sustainable, and data-driven solutions for energy, pharma, and healthcare
• Translate molecular science into usable technology

Thermodynamics, Energy & Molecular Science

How energy, entropy, and molecular interactions govern devices, reactions, and materials.

  • Engineering Thermodynamics
    Energy and entropy balances in open systems; analysis of engines, turbines, compressors, refrigeration, and heat pumps.
  • Applied Physical Chemistry
    Phase equilibrium, vapor–liquid behavior, activity models, electrolyte and nonelectrolyte solutions, and the molecular basis of thermodynamic properties.
  • Thermodynamics and Statistical Mechanics
    Classical and molecular thermodynamics; ensembles, partition functions, equilibrium and stability in chemical and biomolecular systems.
  • Advanced Thermodynamics in Practice
    Practical thermodynamics and kinetics for real processes: energy cycles, phase equilibria, reactor behavior, and multi-reaction kinetics.

Transport, Flow & Multiscale Processes

How momentum, heat, and mass move through fluids, tissues, devices, and reactors.

  • Transport Phenomena I
    Foundations of momentum, heat, and mass transfer; derivation of Navier–Stokes; conservation laws and exact solutions.
  • Transport Phenomena II
    Laminar vs. turbulent flow, boundary layers, convective transfer, boiling, condensation, interphase mass transfer, and industrial correlations.
  • Transport Phenomena in Practice
    Biological and medical transport: oxygen in lungs, blood rheology, shear stress on cells, filtration and membranes, drug movement and uptake in tissue.
  • Advanced Transport Phenomena
    Application of transport theory and kinetics to vascular biology, microcirculation, and deformable cells in low-Reynolds-number flow.

Reaction Engineering & Process Design

Designing chemical and biological transformations, and turning them into scalable systems.

  • Kinetic Processes
    Reaction rate data, homogeneous and heterogeneous kinetics, catalytic surfaces, batch / plug flow / stirred tank reactor design, selectivity, nonisothermal reactors.
  • Chemical & Biomolecular Separations
    Distillation, extraction, gas absorption, ultrafiltration, reverse osmosis, dialysis, adsorption, chromatography — including how separations change for biochemical products.
  • Engineering Principles of Drug Delivery
    Transport-driven delivery of therapeutics; controlled release, targeting, interfacial chemistry, biodistribution, toxicity, and pharmacokinetics.
  • Advanced Topics in Pharmacodynamics
    Project-based exploration of how drugs act in the body, how biological response is modeled, and how therapy can be optimized.

Biological Systems, Cells & Nanomedicine

Where engineering meets living systems, targeted therapy, and the human body.

  • Metabolic Systems Biotechnology
    Systems biology of metabolism: cellular growth, stoichiometric models, metabolic networks, flux analysis, genome-scale metabolic modeling, and bioreactor design.
  • Fundamentals of Cell Bioengineering
    Cell mechanics, cytoskeleton, adhesion, extracellular matrix, signal transduction, mechanobiology, cancer progression, and therapeutic strategies.
  • Supramolecular Materials and Nanomedicine
    Nanocarriers for drug delivery, diagnostic nanoparticles, scaffolds for tissue engineering, and translation of nanoscale materials into clinical technologies.
  • Project in Design: Pharmacodynamics
    Team-based modeling of how drugs act in vivo: bioavailability, spatial and temporal response, and therapeutic impact.
  • Project in Design: Pharmacokinetics
    Modeling of dosing, metabolism, transport across barriers like the blood–brain barrier, tissue storage, and time-resolved drug distribution across organs.

Polymers, Soft Matter & Interfacial Science

Designing responsive materials for energy, manufacturing, sustainability, and medicine.

  • Introduction to Polymeric Materials
    Polymer synthesis, processing, and structure–property relationships for structural and functional polymers, including aerospace, medical, and sustainable materials.
  • Polymer Physics
    Molecular origins of polymer behavior: relaxation, viscoelasticity, fracture, phase transitions, physical aging, and emerging uses in 3D printing and tissue engineering.
  • Polymer Design and Bioconjugation
    Advanced polymer and conjugation chemistry for biomaterials, biointerfaces, smart delivery systems, and engineered tissues.
  • Colloids and Nanoparticles
    Brownian motion, diffusion, aggregation, electrostatics, interfacial forces, and nanoscale behavior in colloidal systems.
  • Interfacial Science with Applications to Nanoscale Systems
    Fluid–fluid, solid–fluid, and solid–solid interfaces; how nanoscale surfaces define performance in sensors, coatings, energy devices, and biomedical systems.

Computation, Modeling & Intelligent Design

Simulation, prediction, and digital design for molecules, devices, and full systems.

  • Software Carpentry
    Practical scientific computing skills: programming, reproducibility, data workflows, and research-grade tooling for modeling and simulation.
  • Computational Protein Structure Prediction and Design
    Protein folding and docking, design of binding sites and interfaces, and de novo protein design using molecular modeling and PyRosetta.
  • Micro- and Nanotechnology
    Micro/nanofabrication methods, scaling laws, analytical techniques, and applications in electronics, optics, biosensing, and implantable systems.
  • Introduction to Nonlinear Dynamics and Chaos
    Real-world nonlinear systems in physics, chemistry, and biology: pattern formation, chaotic behavior, data-driven analysis of dynamic systems.
  • Project in Design: Alternative Energy
    Industry-style team project: modeling a real alternative energy process, evaluating technical performance and economic viability.

Energy, Sustainability & Climate Engineering

Building cleaner energy systems and climate-resilient technologies.

  • Renewable Energy Technologies
    Core science and engineering of alternative and renewable energy systems.
  • Green Engineering, Alternative Energy and CO₂ Capture / Sequestration
    Environmentally responsible process design, carbon capture strategies (solvent, membrane, oxy-fuel), storage pathways, policy and economics of decarbonization, and long-term sustainability.
Chemical & Biomolecular Engineering prepares you to solve problems in energy, health, advanced materials, sustainability, and intelligent process design.
